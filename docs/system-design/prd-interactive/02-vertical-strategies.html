The strategy principle

Every vertical implements a common strategy interface in core-api. This is where "Rooms behave this way, F&B behaves that way" lives — in one place, not in parallel silos. The booking, payment, and ledger flows stay vertical-agnostic.

Strategy interface — three concerns

MethodReturnsDescription
getAvailability(productId, window, quantity) AvailabilityResult How many units are available in the given window
calculatePrice(product, line) Money The price for this BookingLine at booking time (snapshot)
defaultCaptureMode() CaptureMode IMMEDIATE or MANUAL — the payment default for this vertical

Design rules

1

Strategy is a plug-in. The booking service calls the strategy interface; it does not branch on vertical type.

2

defaultCaptureMode() is a default, not a mandate. The caller may explicitly pass a different capture mode on the payment request.

3

No strategy may add endpoints. All capability lives in the shared API surface (HS-05).

4

Price snapshot at booking time. The strategy calculates price once; that value is stored on the BookingLine and never recalculated.

Strategy interface (conceptual)

interface VerticalStrategy {
    AvailabilityResult getAvailability(productId, window, quantity);
    Money              calculatePrice(product, bookingLine);
    CaptureMode        defaultCaptureMode();
}

The strategy is selected by the vertical field on Product. Concrete implementations: RoomsStrategy, SpaStrategy, FoodBeverageStrategy, EventsStrategy.

No strategy adds endpoints

Strategy classes are domain-internal. They do not expose HTTP endpoints. All capability surfaces through the shared API (HS-05).

Rooms strategy

ConcernBehaviour
Availability unitRoom / night. Capacity = number of rooms of a given category per date.
PricingPer-night rate from the Product config; multiplied by nights on the BookingLine.
Default capture modeMANUAL — authorise card at booking; capture at checkout.
Partial captureSupported. E.g. early checkout: authorise full stay, capture actual nights.
Multi-captureOut of scope. One auth → at most one capture.

Why MANUAL for Rooms?

Hotels traditionally hold a card at check-in and charge at check-out, once the actual charges are known (incidentals, early departure, etc.). MANUAL models this: authorise the expected amount, capture the actual amount at departure.

Preference supportspa_therapist preference (e.g. female) can be matched by the MCP layer.

Preference-aware suggestion

The API returns availability alongside stored preferences. The LLM does the matching — not the strategy. The strategy just returns slots; the MCP layer filters based on customer preferences.

F&B (Restaurants) strategy

ConcernBehaviour
Availability unitCovers per service period. Capacity = max covers per period.
PricingPer-cover rate; total is quantity × rate.
Default capture modeIMMEDIATE — auth and capture together at booking.
Service periodsExamples: breakfast 07:00–10:00, lunch 12:00–14:30, dinner 18:00–22:00.

Why IMMEDIATE for F&B?

Restaurant covers are pre-paid at the time of booking. There is no outstanding balance to settle later — the charge is known and final at confirmation.

Standing rule

Frozen contracts may not drift silently. Any deviation a builder must make is recorded here with date, the clause affected, what changed, and why. Until a row is arbitrated and the contract re-frozen, the code is the source of truth for that clause.
