Unified abstraction

Every vertical is the same thing at the domain level: a bookable resource with capacity over time, sold to a customer, paid for, and posted to a ledger. Vertical-specific behaviour lives in per-vertical strategy classes — not in parallel silos.

Authoritative DDL

The DDL is the source of truth for columns, types, constraints, and indexes. Consult contracts/WAVE0_01_SCHEMA.sql directly — this PRD is the conceptual model.

Customer

  • Identity and contact information.
  • Carries an immutable, opaque shopperReference (SHPR-…).
  • Minted once — never changed or exposed as the raw DB id.
  • Sent to the PSP for customer continuity across stays.

CustomerPreference

  • Flexible key/value store, cross-vertical.
  • Examples: floor=high, dietary=vegan, spa_therapist=female.
  • Read by the MCP layer (Wave 2) for preference-aware availability suggestion: the API returns availability + preferences; the LLM does the matching.

Product (polymorphic)

  • Keyed by vertical: ROOMS, SPA, FB, EVENTS.
  • Holds vertical-specific config (e.g. room category, treatment duration, cover count, capacity).
  • A Deluxe Room, a 60-min massage, a dinner slot, and a horse ride are all Products.

Booking / Folio

One Customer + one or more BookingLines. Can span all four verticals (room + spa + dinner = one folio).

FieldTypeMeaning
totalAmountMoneySum of all BookingLine price snapshots
amountPaidMoneySum of all captured payments
amountRefundedMoneySum of all refunds across all payments
balanceDerivedtotalAmount − amountPaid + amountRefunded

Paid signal

Paid = balance == 0. There is no boolean isPaid field.

One Booking → many Payments (partial payments, retried links). A Payment settles exactly one Booking. No many-to-many.

BookingLine

  • Product reference, startTime, endTime (or date for room nights), quantity.
  • Price snapshot — the price at booking time, stored immutably. Price changes after booking do not affect existing lines.
  • Status: CONFIRMED, CANCELLED.
  • Committed bookings consume inventory; cancellations release it immediately.

Inventory

  • Generic capacity per product/slot.
  • Consumed by committed bookings only. No holds/drafts in this POC.
  • The availability seam is kept clean — holds are purely additive later (a hold becomes "another thing that consumes availability").
  • Per-vertical strategies interpret capacity rules (see HS-02).

Domain invariants

1

shopperReference is immutable once minted. Never update it.

2

A BookingLine price snapshot is fixed at booking time. Price changes after booking do not affect existing lines.

3

Inventory is decremented only when a BookingLine moves to CONFIRMED. Cancellation returns capacity immediately.

4

balance == 0 is the canonical paid signal. No redundant boolean.

5

A Booking may have zero Payments (unpaid) — tracked by listUnpaidBookings.

6

A Payment settles exactly one Booking. No many-to-many payment↔booking.

Out of scope

Holds / drafts Multi-currency Real customer auth

Standing rule

Frozen contracts may not drift silently. Any deviation a builder must make is recorded here with date, the clause affected, what changed, and why. Until a row is arbitrated and the contract re-frozen, the code is the source of truth for that clause.
